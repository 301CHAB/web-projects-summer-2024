Early Life

Chase Young was born in Upper Marlboro, Maryland, on April 14, 1999. He began playing football at a young age, attending St. Columba School in Oxon Hill, Maryland, before transferring to St. Vincent Pallotti High School in Laurel, Maryland. At Pallotti, he played multiple positions before focusing on defensive end. In 2015, Young transferred to DeMatha Catholic High School in Hyattsville, Maryland, where he excelled, recording 19 sacks and 27 tackles for loss, helping the team win the WCAC championship.

Recognized as one of the top high school players in the nation, Young was included in the 2016 USA Today All-USA team and named the all-metropolitan defensive football player of the year by The Washington Post. He also participated in the International Bowl and the All-American Bowl.

College Career

Ohio State

Young committed to Ohio State in July 2016, attracted by their family-oriented approach and defensive line coach Larry Johnson. As a freshman in 2017, he recorded 3.5 sacks and 18 tackles. In his sophomore year, despite injuries, he made 10.5 sacks and was named second-team All-Big Ten. As a junior in 2019, Young tied Ohio State records for single-game sacks (4) and tackles for loss (5), ending the season with 16.5 sacks, breaking the school's single-season record.

Young received numerous accolades, including the Bronko Nagurski Trophy, Chuck Bednarik Award, and Ted Hendricks Award. He was also a finalist for the Heisman Trophy, finishing fourth. His achievements led to him being named Big Ten Male Athlete of the Year and he was recognized as a unanimous All-American.

Professional Career

Washington Football Team / Commanders

Young was selected second overall by the Washington Redskins in the 2020 NFL Draft and signed a four-year rookie contract worth $34.56 million. He had an impressive rookie season, winning NFL Defensive Rookie of the Year. In a Week 14 game against the 49ers, Young recorded a sack, two passes defended, a forced fumble, and a fumble recovery which he returned 47 yards for a touchdown.

Injury in 2021

In 2021, Young's season was cut short due to a significant knee injury, tearing his ACL and patellar tendon in Week 10. This required extensive surgery and a lengthy recovery period. The injury was a setback in what was anticipated to be a breakout season for Young.

San Francisco 49ers

In November 2023, Young was traded to the San Francisco 49ers. He recorded 2.5 sacks and played in Super Bowl LVIII, where the 49ers were defeated by the Kansas City Chiefs. Despite the loss, Young's performance was notable, contributing significantly to the team's defense.

Citation: Wikipedia

New Orleans Saints

In March 2024, Young signed a one-year contract with the New Orleans Saints. Shortly after, it was announced that he would undergo neck surgery, which would likely cause him to miss part of the training camp. Young is expected to be a key player for the Saints once he recovers.
